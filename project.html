Rental Mobile App
Casa: find your new home

Finding a rental is stressful for users, and the current systems available to find rentals are outdated and overcomplicated, the process will be made easier for users with a search engine that streamlines and simplifies the process

Objective

Within a team of four we had the objective of developing a mobile application idea of our choosing. We opened the ideas up for debate and quickly decided the rental app would reach a broader audience and would address an issue most are familiar with. Finding a rental property in Australia can be a slow and painful experience for many.

Research

Focusing on finding users’ pain points with the current rental process in Australia. The key research methods we used were competitor analysis, online survey, and one-on-one interviews.
“Finding a rental is stressful for users, and the current systems available to find rentals are outdated and overcomplicated, the process will be made easier for users with a search engine that streamlines and simplifies the process”.
When interviewee’s were asked on their thoughts on the rental process, the response we received were more emotional rather than technical responses. Which tells us that users find the current process stressful and our solution will have to reduce the stress of the rental process

Definition

Our storyboard scenario shows Steffan arriving to Australia as an international student with a short deadline to find long term accommodation, and as his deadline shortens he gets more and more stressed out.
Only for a roommate at the hostel who arrived AFTER him to find a place FIRST. They introduce him to Casa, and within a short amount of time he has arrange inspections, applied and get the keys to his apartment just before the start of his studies

Ideation

After building out our unique angle into the real estate space. We thought about how our users life may be improved by our app choices and features.
Our User Flow is quite a linear process with a few features created by each of us. We each built out the main features of Wishlist, Viewing Booking and Instant Application Features.

Protyping

Paper wireframes were created based off of our user flow, I drew up the design based on our team meetings, we wanted users to be able to quickly swipe through options, similar to dating sites.
The other feature we wanted to develop was a “quick apply” button, as this function would removed majority of the stress of the rental process, unfortunately due to technical reason this is not possible as realtors won’t accept applications from someone who has not viewed the property.
We pivoted our approach to ensure once the user liked a property it streamlined arranging an inspection and had the application ready to send post-inspection.

Testing

Three rounds of usability testing on our prototype were performed, from low fidelity to high fidelity and at all stages the common feedback we received was for the heart icon, as the users still didn’t understand the use of heart icon as Wishlist and after this feedback we realized that it would be better to create icon that is more literal
Which was unexpected. As we had chosen the icon due to their familiarity, the heart often used in shopping apps to mark items for the Wishlist. This also highlights why user testing is important as it shows how the smallest details can make big impact to user’s understanding.
We incorporated the usability testing results into the design, and achieved a product we were really proud of

Conclusion

Our solution to the issues identified in our research is an app named Casa, and the logo incorporates the Rook chess piece, known to be able to cross the board in one direct move if given a clear path. Our goal is to clear the path for renters so they can have an easy direct path to finding their new home mi casa es su casa
Working in this team was really enjoyable, we had an amazing fast-paced creative flow, as we were determined to collaborate, quickly make decisions and learn from each other.
